Dynamic Benthic Pelagic Size Spectrum Model

About

Figure: Schematic of the Dynamic Benthic-Pelagic Size Spectrum Model (DBPM). Environmental inputs (top left) drive the size-structured dynamics of pelagic predators and benthic detritivores. Pelagic predators feed on smaller pelagic and benthic animals; benthic detritivores feed on detritus. Optional components include habitat complexity and size-specific fishing. Arrows indicate flows of biomass and trophic interactions.

This organization develops and maintains the DBPM (Dynamic Benthic Pelagic Size Spectrum Model), a tool for exploring the size structure and dynamics of benthic and pelagic communities in aquatic ecosystems. DBPM is a global application for studying climate change impacts on marine ecosystems.

DBPM is a coupled physical-ecological model that is forced with climate variables and can be applied globally (1 degree grid cells) or aggregated into Large Marine Ecosystems (LMEs) or other regions of interest.
